To demonstrate the progress made by the Test and Learn team, the data that has been transformed and digitised will be incorporated into a visual demo or proof of concept. This demo shows the progress of work and does not show a view of what a potential geospatial register might look like.

This will deliver the following value:

  • More data will be incorporated into the PoC to demonstrate our progress.
  • Improvements made to the visualisation of the PoC will improve the user experience.
  • Stakeholders will need to be able to see the progress being made in Test & Learn. The PoC is our visualisation tool to enable the Team to share this progress.

What did we do?

The PoC built on the earlier Discovery work to demonstrate how HMLR’s geospatial data could be transformed, structured, and used in a modern geospatial environment.

What did we prove?

  • It is possible to convert TMF data reliably – the previous Discovery had already proven conversion works at scale.
  • PoC confirmed the data can be stored, queried, displayed, and interacted with in a modern geospatial tech stack.
  • TMF and historic practices create significant data quality challenges.
  • Matching geospatial and textual data is possible.
  • The current register is highly title centric.
  • The PoC demonstrated the benefits of moving towards a Place-Party-Interest model aligned with LADM.


Significant opportunities for future services

  • Boundary information services (“what do you know about my boundary?”)
  • Spatially navigable interest layers (easements, covenants, rights, restrictions)
  • Better support for caseworkers and external users

To view the working demo, contact Rob Davidson.
